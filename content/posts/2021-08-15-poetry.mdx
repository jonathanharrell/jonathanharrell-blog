> ... How little we<br/>
> had in common. Their whole world had ended,<br/>
> while somewhere ours continued, past the flat<br/>
> voices and the shuffle around the room, past<br/>
> the borders of this town, where the fields fill<br/>
> with the flashlights of so many people<br/>
> looking for each other, flooding the skies<br/>
> here, floors and floors beneath the stars.
> <cite>The Funeral, by Felicity Sheehy (excerpt)</cite>
